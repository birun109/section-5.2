GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.2.18: Non-Text Contrast (WCAG 1.4.11)

Analysis and Requirements

**GIGW Statement**The visual presentation of... User Interface Components and Graphical Objects has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 against adjacent colour(s).
**WCAG Reference****Success Criterion 1.4.11 Non-Text Contrast**
**WCAG Level****Level AA**
**Clause in Short Form**UI Controls & Graphics Must Meet 3:1 Contrast
**Goal**To help users with low vision perceive active interface elements and essential parts of graphics.
**Requirement**The visual information needed to identify controls (borders, fill) and essential parts of graphics must have a minimum contrast of **3:1**.

Example for Success Criteria ✅

Success Example 1: Button Borders and Focus State (3:1)

Scenario: A submit button's visual boundary and its focus indicator.

**Compliance Justification:**

  • **Button Boundary:** The contrast between the button's background (`#007bff`) and the page background (`#f4f4f9`) is well over 3:1, making the button shape perceivable.
  • **Focus Indicator:** When the button is focused (tabbed to), the black outline has a contrast of approximately **8.9:1** against the background, easily meeting the **3:1** requirement.

Success Example 2: Essential Graphical Object Contrast

Scenario: A bar chart where the bars convey essential data.

(The bars are the graphical object required for understanding.)

**Compliance Justification:** The dark blue bars (`#0056b3`) have a contrast ratio of approximately **4.8:1** against the light gray chart background (`#eee`). Since these bars are **required to understand the content** (the relative size of the data), they must meet the 3:1 ratio.

Example for Failure Criteria ❌

Failure Example 1: Low-Contrast Input Field Border

Scenario: A user input field whose visual boundary is too faint to meet 3:1 contrast.


**Compliance Justification:** The border color of the input field (`#AAAAAA`) on the white background (`#FFFFFF`) has a contrast ratio of only **1.6:1**. Users with low vision may not be able to identify the shape and extent of the input field, meaning the visual information required to identify the component fails the **3:1** requirement.

Failure Example 2: Low-Contrast Focus Indicator

Scenario: The author changes the browser's default focus ring to a color that is almost white.

**Compliance Justification:** The focus indicator is an **essential state indicator** and must meet 3:1 contrast against the adjacent color (the page background). If the focus ring color is very light (e.g., `#EEEEEE`) on a white page, the resulting contrast is approximately **1.05:1**, failing the 3:1 minimum for UI components.

Summary of Conformity

WCAG Reference WCAG Level Key Action for Compliance
SC 1.4.11 Non-Text Contrast AA Ensure the visual information (borders, fill, shadow, color difference) required to identify **active controls** (buttons, inputs, links, focus rings) and **essential graphics** (bars, lines, key icons) has a minimum contrast ratio of **3:1** against the adjacent background or element. Check your custom focus rings!
