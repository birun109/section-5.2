GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.2.26: Three Flashes or Below Threshold (WCAG 2.3.1)

Analysis and Requirements

**WCAG Reference****Success Criterion 2.3.1 Three Flashes or Below Threshold**
**WCAG Level****Level A**
**Clause in Short Form**No More Than 3 Flashes Per Second
**Goal**To protect users with photosensitive epilepsy from seizure-inducing visual content.
**Requirement**Content must not flash more than **three times** in any one-second period. If it does, the flashing area must be small enough or the contrast/color change low enough to stay below the defined **flash thresholds**.

Example for Success Criteria ✅

Success Example 1: Compliant Flash Rate

Scenario: An alert box needs to draw attention to a critical message using a gentle flash.

Flash (3x/sec)

**Compliance Justification:** The flashing effect is designed to flash at a rate of exactly **three times per second** (or less). This meets the **Frequency Limit** rule of SC 2.3.1, ensuring the content is safe for users with photosensitive epilepsy. (Note: The CSS animation simulates a step-end change every 0.33 seconds, resulting in three full cycles per second.)

Success Example 2: Below Threshold Flash Area (Conceptual)

Scenario: A large data visualization requires a status indicator that blinks frequently (e.g., 5 times per second).

The indicator is a single blinking pixel or a small square area that is less than $10\%$ of the screen view or less than $21,800$ square pixels, when viewed at $1024 \times 768$ resolution.

**Compliance Justification:** The content exceeds the frequency limit (more than 3 flashes per second), but it is compliant because the total area of the flashing content is **below the General Flash Threshold**. This is often used for small, non-obtrusive status lights.

Example for Failure Criteria ❌

Failure Example 1: Excessive Flash Rate

Scenario: A developer creates a warning signal that rapidly blinks between different colors to create urgency.

DANGER: FLASHING 4x/SECOND

**Compliance Justification:** The content is flashing **four times per second** or more. This rate exceeds the WCAG limit of three flashes per second and would pose a significant seizure risk, failing the **Frequency Limit** rule of SC 2.3.1. The flashing element is also large, potentially exceeding the General Flash Threshold.

Failure Example 2: Large Red Flash (Exceeds Red Flash Threshold)

Scenario: An emergency alert occupies more than $10\%$ of the screen and flashes between bright white and primary red, three times per second.

**Large Red Alert:** Even though the frequency is compliant (3x/second), the large area of high-intensity red flashing (white to pure red) can still trigger seizures because red is a particularly sensitive color.

**Compliance Justification:** Flashing involving saturated red is especially hazardous and has a stricter limit (the **Red Flash Threshold**). Even if the frequency is at the maximum allowed (3 times per second), if the flashing content is large and involves significant changes in the luminance of saturated red, it can still be non-compliant due to the intensity and area.

Summary of Conformity

WCAG Reference WCAG Level Key Action for Compliance
SC 2.3.1 Three Flashes or Below Threshold A **Developer Rule:** **NEVER** exceed a rate of **three full flashes per one-second period** for any non-trivial element on the screen. If flashing is unavoidable, either keep the rate low or ensure the flashing element is very small (below the General Flash and Red Flash thresholds).
