GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.3.2: Audio Control (WCAG 1.4.2)

Analysis and Requirements

**GIGW Statement**If any audio on a Web page plays automatically for more than 3 seconds, either a mechanism is available to pause or stop the audio, or a mechanism is available to control audio volume independently...
**WCAG Reference****Success Criterion 1.4.2 Audio Control**
**WCAG Level****Level A**
**Clause in Short Form**Stop Auto-Playing Audio Over 3 Seconds
**Goal**To ensure screen reader speech output can be heard clearly without interference, and to prevent auditory distraction.
**Requirement**Auto-playing audio lasting > 3 seconds must have an immediate mechanism to **Pause/Stop** or control the **Volume** independently.

Success Examples ‚úÖ

Success Example 1: Immediate and Accessible Stop Button

Scenario: A government portal homepage loads with a 15-second background ambient music loop.

**Compliance Justification:** An easily locatable and accessible **Stop button** is prominently displayed and activated immediately upon page load. The button is correctly labeled using `aria-label` for screen reader users, providing an immediate mechanism to stop the audio, fulfilling the requirement.

Success Example 2: Short Auto-Playing Audio

Scenario: A simple, two-second "welcome chime" plays when the user logs in.

The **audio clip is exactly 2.5 seconds** long and does not loop.

**Compliance Justification:** The audio stops playing on its own **within 3 seconds**. Since the duration constraint is met, no explicit Stop/Pause/Volume control is required for the audio element itself.

Failure Examples ‚ùå

Failure Example 1: Missing or Hidden Control

Scenario: A 20-second informational video plays automatically upon entering the page, but the controls are hidden or delayed.

The video is auto-played, but the controls only appear when the user hovers their mouse over the video player (or are visually obscured behind a carousel element).

**Compliance Justification:** The video plays for **more than 3 seconds**, and keyboard users or screen reader users may not be able to immediately stop the audio since the controls are **not immediately visible or programmatically available** at the beginning of the page flow. This interference blocks access to screen reader output.

Failure Example 2: Volume Control Only Affects System Volume

Scenario: Background sound (10 seconds long) starts playing, and the only way to silence it is using the computer's system volume control.

The page offers no on-screen control to turn off the audio or adjust its volume independently.

**Compliance Justification:** The control must be available to **control audio volume independently from the overall system volume**. Forcing the user to mute their entire computer just to read the webpage fails this requirement, as it silences their screen reader or other necessary applications.

Summary of Conformity

WCAG Reference WCAG Level Key Action for Compliance
SC 1.4.2 Audio Control A If any audio plays automatically, it **must be less than 3 seconds long**. If it is longer, an **immediate, clearly accessible (visible and keyboard-operable) mechanism** to **Pause/Stop** the audio or control its **volume independently** of the system volume must be provided.
