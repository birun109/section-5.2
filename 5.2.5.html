GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.2.5: Live Captions (WCAG 1.2.4)

Clause: Live Audio Content in Synchronised Media

Requirement: Captions must be provided for all live video streams and broadcasts to ensure real-time access for deaf or hard-of-hearing users.

✅ Success Example: Live Stream with Real-Time Captions

Media: A live press conference being streamed online.

LIVE PRESS CONFERENCE

**[PM]**: ...and the new infrastructure plan will commence in Q1. **[SOUND: Applause]**

The captions are generated in real-time and include necessary non-speech information (**[SOUND: Applause]**), which confirms compliance. Developers must ensure the live streaming platform supports this feature (e.g., using a professional captioning service).

❌ Failure Example 1: Missing Captions Entirely

Media: A live emergency broadcast about a severe weather event.

LIVE EMERGENCY ALERT

Crucial, real-time information (e.g., evacuation routes, specific warnings) is only conveyed via audio. Without captions, deaf users are completely excluded from accessing this vital, time-sensitive content, resulting in a Level AA failure.

❌ Failure Example 2: Non-Synchronized or Delayed Captions

Media: A live Q&A session where captions appear minutes after the speaker finishes their sentence.

LIVE Q&A SESSION

**Caption Text**: (This caption relates to the speaker's statement from 30 seconds ago.)

While captions exist, if they are severely delayed or unsynchronized (e.g., due to poor automated system quality), they fail to serve the purpose of **real-time accessibility**, making the flow of the conversation incomprehensible to the user.

Summary of Conformity

GIGW Statement WCAG Reference WCAG Level Required Action
5.2.5 (Live Captions) SC 1.2.4 AA Government organizations and developers must ensure that all live synchronized media is provided with accurate, synchronized, real-time captions.
