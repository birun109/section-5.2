GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.2.25: Pause, Stop, Hide (WCAG 2.2.2)

Analysis and Requirements

**WCAG Reference****Success Criterion 2.2.2 Pause, Stop, Hide**
**WCAG Level****Level A**
**Clause in Short Form**Control Over Moving Content
**Goal**To give users control over automatically moving or rapidly changing content.
**Requirement (Moving/Scrolling/Blinking)**Must have a **Pause, Stop, or Hide** mechanism if the movement is automatic, lasts $\ge 5$ seconds, and runs in parallel with other content.
**Requirement (Auto-Updating)**Must have a **Pause, Stop, Hide** mechanism or **control over the update frequency** if the update is automatic and runs in parallel with other content.

Example for Success Criteria ✅

Success Example 1: Controllable Carousel (Moving Content)

Scenario: An image carousel or news slider that rotates slides automatically every 5 seconds (runs for more than 5 seconds).

Slide 1: This text moves automatically!

**Compliance Justification:** This meets the criterion by providing a clear **Pause** button. The movement is automatic and lasts longer than 5 seconds, so user control is essential for users to read the content on the slides.

Success Example 2: Adjustable Data Stream (Auto-Updating Content)

Scenario: A dashboard widget displaying real-time stock prices or traffic data.

Latest Data Refresh: 0 seconds ago

**Compliance Justification:** The system offers a **Pause** mechanism and control over the **Frequency** of the updates. Both satisfy the requirement for auto-updating content, allowing users to stabilize the data stream to read the information.

Example for Failure Criteria ❌

Failure Example 1: Non-Controllable Scrolling News Ticker

Scenario: A non-essential news ticker running perpetually at the top of the page without user controls.

*** Urgent News Update: Developers forgot to add a pause button! This scrolling text runs forever and cannot be stopped. *** This constant movement can distract users trying to read the main content below. ***

**Compliance Justification:** The scrolling text is **Moving** (scrolling), **starts automatically**, **lasts more than 5 seconds** (indefinitely), and is presented **in parallel** with other content. The **lack of any mechanism** to pause, stop, or hide the element makes this a failure of SC 2.2.2.

Failure Example 2: Auto-Updating Stock Ticker with Fixed Rate

Scenario: A stock price widget updates every 10 seconds, but the user cannot slow it down or stop it.

Stock Price: ₹ 100.00 (Updates every 10 seconds, NO control provided.)

**Compliance Justification:** The auto-updating information is presented in parallel with other content, yet the developer has provided **no mechanism** to pause it, stop it, hide it, or control the frequency. A user relying on a screen reader might miss the crucial stock value before it refreshes, leading to a failure of SC 2.2.2.

Summary of Conformity

WCAG Reference WCAG Level Key Action for Compliance
SC 2.2.2 Pause, Stop, Hide A **Moving/Blinking/Scrolling:** Provide a **Pause/Stop/Hide** button for all non-essential automatic movement lasting $\ge 5$ seconds. **Auto-Updating:** Provide a **Pause/Stop/Hide** button or a **Frequency Control** for all non-essential automatic data updates.
